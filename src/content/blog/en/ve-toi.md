I am a curious person who asks questions and seeks answers. My personality formed from talking to myself and explaining things to myself throughout my childhood. It was only in middle school that I had more opportunities to access the knowledge I needed via a computer. That sparked even more searching for answers. Each time I find an answer or understand something, I apply it to life. Then I rest. After resting I ask questions again from the knowledge I just learned. And so I keep moving forward.

> How about you? What motivation brought you here?

I also like playing games. When I get bored, I roll into studying. When I see something strange or striking, I get curious, try it, and find it's easier to approach than I thought, though there is more new stuff than I expected. That keeps me pursuing it until I no longer can pose any new questions.

"Why do I study programming?"

I did it for ~~money~~, honestly out of boredom. Finishing a project I wanted sometimes kills the excitement. Later programming became a way to build the things I yearn for, not just to learn. This time I learn math and graphics because of programming and then stop. Oh, and working with a team, a small group, takes away the rest of my curiosity. At least for this time.

"Why do I study graphics?"

It’s easy to approach, easy to explore, and easy to leverage visually. Plus you learn science too, which is great and convenient for asking questions about things around me.
